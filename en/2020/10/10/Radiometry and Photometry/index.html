Radiometry and Photometry

Last Update：August 6, 2021 am

Radiometry is the subject of measuring all kinds of electromagnetic radiation including visible light. And photometry is the subject of measuring the intensity of visible light perceived by human eyes. Since human eyes have different sensitivity to different wavelengths of light, only by weighting the radiation power of different wavelengths with photometric function can we accurately describe the intensity of light perceived by human beings.

Radiometry

Radiant Flux

In radiometry, radiant flux or radiant power $\Phi_\mathrm{e}$ is the radiant energy emitted, reflected, transmitted or received per unit time, the unit of which is Watt $\mathrm W$.

Figure 1 - Radiant Flux

As we can see, the point light source in the figure above emits $11$ arrows. So its radiation flux is $11\mathrm W$. We can also consider a surface that emits or receives light, and its radiation flux is the number of arrows emitted or received. If we further assume that the arrows have various colors, then can be imagined as the number of arrows with the color $\lambda$.
